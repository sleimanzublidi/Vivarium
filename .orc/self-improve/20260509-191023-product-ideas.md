# 20260509-191023 — Product Ideas

## 1. Surface Pet Pack Import Issues
**Value:** 4
**Feasibility:** 4
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 16 (2 × 4 + 4 + 4)
**Description:** When a pet pack cannot be installed or loaded, Vivarium should show a clear, user-facing issue list from the menu or tank UI. Each issue should name the pack, explain what went wrong in plain language, and suggest the likely fix, such as checking the manifest, image format, dimensions, or duplicate pet identifier.
**Rationale:** Pet packs are a core customization promise, but invalid packs currently risk feeling like they simply disappeared. Clear import feedback helps users recover without searching console logs, increases confidence in third-party packs, and makes pack authoring less trial-and-error.

## 2. Opt-in Attention Alerts for Waiting or Failed Sessions
**Value:** 4
**Feasibility:** 3
**Safety:** 3
**Composite:** 14 (2 × 4 + 3 + 3)
**Description:** Add an explicit menu preference for desktop alerts when an agent is waiting for user input or has failed. Alerts should be off by default, easy to enable, and limited to actionable states so users are not spammed during normal tool activity.
**Rationale:** The tank can be hidden, covered, or on another display, which makes the most important states easy to miss. Opt-in alerts turn Vivarium from a passive visual companion into a practical attention aid while preserving a quiet default experience.

## 3. Manual Refresh for Externally Added Pet Packs
**Value:** 3
**Feasibility:** 4
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 14 (2 × 3 + 4 + 4)
**Description:** Add a visible "Refresh Pets" action that reloads pet packs copied or edited outside the app and reports how many packs were added, updated, rejected, or left unchanged. Drag-and-drop installation should remain the fastest path, but manual refresh should cover users who manage packs through Finder, scripts, or sync tools.
**Rationale:** Restarting the app to discover externally managed packs is easy to miss and makes customization feel unreliable. A manual refresh is smaller and safer than full filesystem watching while still giving users an obvious recovery path when a copied pack does not appear.
