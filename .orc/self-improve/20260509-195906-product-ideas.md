# 20260509-195906 — Product Ideas

## 1. Privacy mode for public screens
**Value:** 5
**Feasibility:** 4
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 18
**Description:** Add a clear privacy mode that hides or generalizes sensitive text in pet balloons, session summaries, and any visible status surfaces while preserving the pet's state animations. Users should be able to toggle it quickly from the menu, and the mode should make states understandable without showing prompts, command details, project names, or error text.
**Rationale:** Vivarium is intentionally glanceable and can sit above other windows, which also means it can expose private work during screen sharing, demos, pairing, or coworking. A privacy mode keeps the product useful in public contexts and reduces the risk that users hide or quit the app whenever sensitive work starts.

## 2. Inspect latest session details from the pet
**Value:** 4
**Feasibility:** 4
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 16
**Description:** Let users open a compact details view for a pet or active session showing the current state, agent, project label, latest meaningful message, and last update time, with a simple copy action for troubleshooting. The visible tank can stay concise, while the details view provides the full context behind truncated balloons and attention states.
**Rationale:** Short balloons are good for ambient awareness, but they can leave users unsure why a pet is waiting or failed. A lightweight details view turns the pet from a decorative indicator into an actionable status surface without requiring users to hunt through terminal output or system logs.

## 3. Launch at login and running-state confidence
**Value:** 4
**Feasibility:** 3
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 15
**Description:** Add an obvious way to keep Vivarium running after restarts, such as a launch-at-login control plus menu copy that confirms whether background tracking is active. The product should make it clear that hiding the tank keeps tracking alive, while quitting the app stops future pet updates until it is launched again.
**Rationale:** Hooks only produce visible pets when the app is alive, so users who reboot, quit the app accidentally, or confuse hiding with quitting can experience silent non-updates and misdiagnose setup as broken. Keeping the app running by default or making that state explicit improves first-run confidence and day-to-day reliability.
