# 20260509-193807 — Product Ideas

## 1. Make crowded tanks inspectable
**Value:** 4
**Feasibility:** 4
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 16
**Description:** When more sessions are active than can fit in the tank, make the overflow indicator interactive. A click or hover should show the hidden sessions with their agent, project label, and current state, and offer a clear way to bring a hidden session into view or jump to its existing menu entry.
**Rationale:** The current overflow count tells users that something is hidden, but not what is hidden or whether one of those sessions needs attention. Users running several agents at once can miss waiting, failed, or recently active work because the tank compresses the least visible sessions into an opaque count. Making overflow inspectable preserves the calm default view while keeping multi-session workflows trustworthy.

## 2. Notify users when an agent needs attention
**Value:** 4
**Feasibility:** 3
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 15
**Description:** Add an opt-in notification preference for sessions that enter waiting or failed states while the tank is hidden, covered, or not in the user's visual focus. Notifications should identify the agent and project in plain language and should avoid noisy alerts for normal running, thinking, or idle transitions.
**Rationale:** Vivarium is most useful when it surfaces moments that need user action. If the tank is hidden, behind other windows, or on another display, a permission prompt or failed tool can sit unnoticed even though the pet has the right state. A conservative attention-only notification closes that gap without turning every agent event into an interruption.

## 3. Add window interference controls
**Value:** 3
**Feasibility:** 4
**Safety:** 4
**Composite:** 14
**Description:** Give users simple controls for how much the tank participates in the desktop: a menu toggle for whether it stays above other windows, plus a low-friction way to temporarily click through or reduce interaction when it covers terminal or editor content.
**Rationale:** A floating animated companion can become distracting if it blocks text selection, terminal clicks, or screen sharing. Users should not have to choose between quitting the app and fighting the window. Clear interference controls make the product easier to keep running all day, which is essential for a companion that depends on continuous session tracking.
